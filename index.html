About Me

Humble Beginnings

I got my start at the wee age of 15 (Back in 2012) creating educational Unity Games for my high-school homework assignments. Why make a powerpoint when I could make a game? That was where I first discovered my love of Javascript and UI/UX design. From here I branched into learning several different technologies, among them Autodesk 3DSmax for Modeling and Photoshop for UI and Texture creation. Most of these were hobby projects I made with some friends and as a result I learned my coding fundamentals at a fairly young age. I also had a brief stint in 3D Animation and was hired by a company to work on an Award Winning Documentary Dark Forest Black Fly. The documentary was for raising awareness on a harrowing disease in Africa called river blindness. I was awarded an Outsanding Achievement in animation by the NATASSoutheast (Same people that hand out Emmys) for my work on the project and you can see that documentary Here. You can also see my adorable first foray into my humble beginnings as a developer here!

College

I then decided to take my passion to college and get my Bachelors in Computer Game Development and Design. This program was Computer Science focused and I got my Minor in CS, as well as a focus on Software Engineering. Desipite being Video Game focused I still learned core principles of Software Engineering, AI Development (With a brief touch into Neural Networks), SQL, Java, C++, and of course plenty of Code Theory. In my spare time I attended Game Jam's twice a year, always looking to further my skills. Winnng the local 2018 game jam in my Junior Year. The culmination of my work was my Senior Project which was an intense 6 month video game titled Vesta Absolution with a team 3. This project was a huge endeavor with me being the lead programmer and having a hand in every single system we created. I learned about Git and version control which, hoo-boy, with students is a task and a half. The meat and potatoes for this game however was my, custom OpenGL shader programming, Animation Rigging and Animation State Management programming, Character Controllers, Level Design, 3D Character Modelling, and AI. Many of these systems I learned and improved on as we progressed. We also did weekly sprints following an Agile Methodology, adhering to the core principles. You can view and play this project here!

The Transition to Web Development

Then the Plague happened. Everyone had their own hiccups and I won't get into that but I took this time to take a break and explore other options for my career. From there I realized that I wanted to have a stable career, while still doing what I was passionate about, coding to improve lives. I then attended a Coding MERN Full Stack Bootcamp at Georgia Institute of Technology to make a transition into the Dev side of things and pick up a tech stack. I excelled in this program, quickly being recognized as one of the most talented in the Cohort, and was offered a TA position after just two weeks of the course. One notable project had me as a Project Manager with 3 students who had never coded prior, and I guided them on making a website from scratch using HTML, CSS, Javascript, Bootstrap, along with Marvel API and Google Books API. While most of the code wasn't directly mine, I had a hand in almost all of it. I learned a lot about what it takes to manage a team. You need concrete coding standard. You need documentation and ensure that each of your members is adhering to documentation. And most of all you need a "We can do this" attitude. If you believe you can do it, you can get it done. You can see that project here.

Where I Currently Am

I am currently finishing the course and working with two other extremely talented engineers to create our own Web App E-Commerce Bulletin to show off the skills I acquired in the camp but as this is still a work in progress you'll have to stay tuned. I am looking to jump start my career get my foot in the door with my first CS job. This is my passion. I want to create and make applications that improve peoples lives. And mostly, I'm a problem solver always looking to learn, improve, and grow. I have an interest in web development but it's more than that. As you can see with my early work, I prioritized creating things that educate and improve peoples lives in a meaningful way. So while if you are reading this know that I am not only interested in Web Dev but mainly applying my skills as a programmer and artist to create worthwhile applications.
